# FORD UNIVERSAL OWNER MANUAL - CONSOLIDATED KNOWLEDGE BASE

## 1. DASHBOARD WARNING SYMBOLS AND INDICATORS
[COLOR SYSTEM OVERVIEW]
- RED: Danger or serious malfunction. Stop the vehicle safely and turn off the engine immediately.
- AMBER/YELLOW: System fault or action required soon. Drive with caution.
- GREEN/BLUE: System status or informational; indicates a feature is currently active.

[RED WARNINGS - URGENT]
- [BRAKE SYSTEM]: Indicates the parking brake is on or brake fluid is low. If it stays on after releasing the brake, seek immediate service.
- [ENGINE COOLANT TEMPERATURE]: Engine is overheating. Safely pull over and shut off the engine. Do not open the coolant cap while hot.
- [ENGINE OIL PRESSURE]: Critically low oil pressure. Shut off the engine immediately to prevent permanent damage.
- [CHARGING SYSTEM]: Battery or charging system fault. Turn off unnecessary electrical equipment and seek service.
- [AIRBAG SYSTEM]: Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) fault. Airbags may not deploy in an accident.
- [DOOR/HOOD/LIFTGATE AJAR]: One or more openings are not securely closed.

[AMBER WARNINGS - CAUTION]
- [WRENCH SYMBOL]: Powertrain, AWD, or 4WD fault. A problem is detected in the engine or drivetrain.
- [CHECK ENGINE / SERVICE ENGINE SOON]: Malfunction in the engine or emissions system.
- [ABS WARNING]: Anti-lock braking is disabled; standard braking remains functional.
- [STABILITY CONTROL]: Flashes when active on slippery surfaces; remains lit if there is a system fault.
- [LOW TIRE PRESSURE (TPMS)]: One or more tires are below safe pressure levels. Check and inflate to the PSI on the door jamb sticker.
- [LOW FUEL]: Indicates fuel level is low (typically 1/16th to 1/8th of a tank remaining).
- [BLIS/CROSS TRAFFIC]: Indicates a fault in the blind spot or radar-sensing systems.

[GREEN/BLUE - STATUS]
- [CRUISE CONTROL]: System is active or a speed has been set.
- [AUTO START-STOP]: Green 'A' indicates the engine has shut down to save fuel.
- [LANE KEEPING]: Indicates the system is active and tracking lane markings.
- [HIGH BEAM]: Blue light indicates high beam headlights are active.

## 2. MAINTENANCE AND SERVICE REMINDERS
[INTELLIGENT OIL-LIFE MONITOR]
The vehicle calculates oil change intervals based on engine use and environment.
- 'Oil Change Required' message: Change oil within 500 miles (800 km).
- Note: The monitor must be manually reset after every oil change.

[MONTHLY USER CHECKS]
- Check engine oil level using the dipstick.
- Verify function of all interior and exterior lights.
- Inspect tires for proper pressure and uneven wear.
- Check windshield washer fluid level.

[SIX-MONTH CHECKS]
- Inspect battery terminals for corrosion; clean if necessary.
- Check safety belts and seat latches for proper operation.
- Inspect the spare tire air pressure.

[LONG-TERM SERVICE ITEMS]
- Replace engine air filter: Every 30,000 miles (approx. 48,000 km).
- Replace spark plugs: Every 100,000 miles (approx. 160,000 km).
- Change transmission fluid: Every 150,000 miles (approx. 240,000 km).

## 3. BASIC TROUBLESHOOTING AND PROCEDURES
[ENGINE FAILS TO START]
1. Ensure the gear selector is fully in Park (P).
2. For push-button start: Ensure the brake pedal is fully depressed.
3. If the 'No Key Detected' message appears, place the key fob in the backup slot (usually in the center console).
4. Check battery terminals for loose or corroded connections.

[ENGINE OVERHEATING WHILE DRIVING]
1. Turn off the air conditioning.
2. Set the heater to the maximum temperature and highest fan speed.
3. If the temperature does not decrease, pull over safely and shut down the engine.

[JUMP STARTING PROCEDURE]
1. Connect red (+) cable to the positive terminal of the discharged battery.
2. Connect the other red (+) end to the booster battery's positive terminal.
3. Connect black (-) cable to the booster battery's negative terminal.
4. Connect the final black (-) cable to a grounded metal part of the engine (away from the battery and fuel lines) in the vehicle being jumped.

[BATTERY RELEARN PROCESS]
If the battery was recently disconnected or replaced, the engine and transmission may idle roughly or shift oddly. Drive the vehicle normally for 5-10 miles (8-16 km) to allow the computer systems to recalibrate.

[POWER LIFTGATE ISSUES]
If the liftgate fails to open or close:
1. Check that the vehicle is in Park (P).
2. Ensure there are no obstructions in the liftgate path.
3. Check if the battery voltage is low.
